Archive Record: The Great Hall

The Great Hall serves as the Academy’s seat of ceremony, judgement and formal assembly. Six chairs flank the great central seat, each bound to the legacy of the Houses.

Its timber floor is older than most records admit. The Owls preserve fragments of architectural notes suggesting the hall was never built merely to gather students, but to guard something beneath them.

Deeper structural records remain sealed by order of the Royal Archive.
